The challenge

An estate approached us to help protect their intellectual property across YouTube, where thousands of channels were using their content without permission. Managing the rights to a large catalog of legacy material is inherently complex — especially when it’s spread across numerous creator-run channels, split into clips, reactions, and foreign-language re-uploads.

Widespread misuse

27,000 unauthorized uploads across YouTube, ranging from full episodes to short clips, with no mechanism for the estate to monetize any of them.

Content detection gap

YouTube’s standard Content ID missed audio-only and lecture-style content. The estate needed deeper detection to catch every variation.

Estate management complexity

Rights holders needed real-time insight across a large library without hiring a dedicated ops team or disrupting creators acting in good faith.

Our solution

01

Audio-only Content ID tool

We worked directly with YouTube to gain exclusive access to, and help improve, a new audio-only Content ID tool — enabling us to accurately track and claim the estate’s catalog where visual fingerprinting missed.

02

Custom rightsholder dashboard

We built a proprietary dashboard specifically designed for rights holders, allowing them to track all content, manage claims & takedowns, and view real-time revenue reporting — without leaving a single interface.

03

Creator licensing ecosystem

Rather than blanket-block every re-upload, we built an ecosystem where creators could engage with the estate’s catalog legitimately — through a sustainable revenue-share model that rewards fan channels doing it right.

04

Content guidelines & policy

We codified clear content guidelines so creators understood exactly how they could use the estate’s work, what counted as fair use, and what the licensing terms were — reducing disputes before they happened.
